About Me

I am a Research Scientist at Snap Research. My research spans challenging problems in the space of misbehavior and abuse on web platforms, and is aimed towards building a safer web by improving platform integrity and user safety. More broadly, I am interested in modeling, characterizing and understanding user behavior by leveraging techniques in data mining, machine learning, network analysis, and more.

Before I joined Snap Research, I defended my PhD thesis in the Computer Science Department at Carnegie Mellon University, where I worked on discovering and modeling various types of abusive online behaviors as anomaly detection problems in large networks. I was very fortunate to have been advised by Christos Faloutsos.

Prior to this, I received my B.S. in Computer Science from the Department of Computer Science at North Carolina State University. My undergraduate research focused on reduction, indexing and storage of large-scale scientific data, and was supervised by Nagiza Samatova.
